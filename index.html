About Almaty

Almaty is not just a place on the map but a living history, carefully preserving its traditions woven into the majestic mountains of the Zailiysky Alatau. The capital of Kazakhstan until 1997, Almaty embodies a blend of cultural richness and modern spirit. The unique character of the city is felt in every street, reflecting the diversity of ethnic groups and historical periods. Modern skyscrapers surrounded by snow-capped mountains coexist with ancient architectural monuments, creating a unique urban landscape.

City History

Medieval Almaty was a crossroads of trade routes connecting East and West along the Silk Road. This city became a cultural melting pot where ideas and traditions of various peoples merged. Over time, Almaty experienced different eras, preserving traces of historical events in its architecture and customs. In the 20th century, the city became part of Soviet history, transforming from a Kazakh cultural center into an industrial hub. Today, Almaty is a modern metropolis with a highly developed infrastructure, yet its streets and monuments remain living witnesses of a rich history that it proudly carries into the future.
